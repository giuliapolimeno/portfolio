The project involved the creation of a new brand identity for the UNESCO World Heritage Sites of the Necropoli of Cerveteri and Tarquinia. Through a targeted communication strategy and the design of various artifacts, the goal was to emphasize the Etruscan belief that tombs serve as a continuation of life, reflecting a joyful and carefree existence. While the necropolis may appear as a series of vegetation-covered domes and tufa structures, there is much more beneath the surface. This concept is visually represented in the new logo, which incorporates the symbol of the sun, embodying the idea of life and the Etruscans’ practice of granting a second life to the deceased within their tombs.
Additionally, a prototype for a mobile application was developed to guide visitors through the necropolises, enhancing their experience by providing context and insights into the significance of these ancient sites. This project was realized during the Visual Communication course, showcasing the importance of effectively conveying the rich cultural heritage of the Etruscans.
