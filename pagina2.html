Venezia all'Opera
Project info

Year
2023
Developed in the "Laboratorio di Progettazione di artefatti e sistemi complessi" and published in RAI Accessibilità site, Venezia all’Opera is a museum accessibility project that adapts the essence of Venice for all audiences, including those with visual and hearing impairments. Drawing from Canaletto’s iconic works, such as Veduta del Bacino di San Marco dalla Punta della Dogana and Veduta del Canal Grande verso la Punta della Dogana, da Campo San Vio from the Pinacoteca di Brera, the project transforms these scenes into immersive audiovisual and tactile formats. The audiovisual artifact brings Canaletto’s Venice to life through a theatrical narrative in which the artist “speaks” to the viewer, guiding them through the city as he envisioned it. This narrative approach uses Venice’s canals and architecture as stage backdrops, creating a vivid performance where each scene element plays its part under the careful direction of the artist.
For tactile engagement, the project includes shaped MDF panels that allow visually impaired users to feel the spatial depth and composition of Canaletto’s paintings. These modular, smooth panels can be layered, focusing touch on the precise contours of each architectural form. The tactile booklet combines black ink and Braille text to be accessible for both sighted and visually impaired visitors, with raised illustrations aligned to the text for clarity. UV-printed Braille and spiral binding ensure durability and easy exploration, creating an inclusive, multisensory experience.
