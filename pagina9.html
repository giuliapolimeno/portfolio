Musica viva
Project info
2D Animation
Year
2023
This animation, inspired by the 1958 Zürich Tonhalle, Musica Viva poster by J. M. Brockmann, was created for a Complex Systems Lab course. The animation brings the poster’s geometric order to life by introducing two rebellious rectangles, which disrupt the poster’s symmetry by immersing themselves in the quintessential chaos of a bustling cityscape. These rectangles, one black and one red, animate the tension of pursuit as the black rectangle continuously strives to catch up to the red. The animation embraces the improvisational spirit of jazz, a genre synonymous with the cultural vibrancy of urban life. Just as jazz clubs offer an escape from routine, the rectangles break free from the confines of geometric order, embodying improvisation and energy. The soundtrack is drawn from The New Yorker Fest 2021 spot, which inspired the piece.
